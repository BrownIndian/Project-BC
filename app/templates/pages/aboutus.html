About Us

Who are we?

By 2021, it is forecasted that 77% of jobs will require significant tecnological skills. We are a group of students that believes no student should be left behind in this revolutionizing world. Project Ember is a student-driven non-profit organization that aims to bring a technical education to underprivileged Bay Area students. Our team consists of five motivated students and teachers from Washington High School in Fremont, California.

What do we do?

Through both teaching and philanthropy, we strive to increase technological literacy for the less fortunate. In the past, we have held computer science and math-related classes for underprivileged students in the Fremont, Union City, and Oakland areas. In total, we have been able to positively impact over 300 learners.

What do we hope to accomplish?

In the future, we hope to continue our computer-science and math teachings. We have also recently partnered with Bay Area Title One Schools so we are able to host a hackathon primarily for (but not limited to) underprivileged students who are passionate about technology.
