Peer to Peer Teaching

Already, we have been privileged enough to mentor over 300 underprivileged students in the San Francisco Bay Area, providing free coding and math “boot-camp” style classes. Our classes have taught a variety of subjects, including but not limited to HTML, Python, Java, and Calculus. From the Fremont Main Library to Oakland City Library, we are always looking for new venues to hold our classes! Since the coronavirus has closed down many public libraries, we are transitioning to a distance learning model with our classes being held on Zoom to ensure that every student will still have access to a robust technical education.

Student-Made Projects

Our student made courses and projects are a great way for a learner to become assimilated into a new field they may have never encountered before. Designed by students who have gone through the process of learning their respective subjects, the courses are created with the intention of being easy to follow and understand. With our team and reach quickly expanding, we are excited to announce that we will be uploading new XCode, Java, Artificial Intelligence, and Multivariate Calculus courses very soon!

Dedication to Philanthropy

Ember Initiative is a nonprofit organization and our team is always looking for new and unique ways to give back to our community. With the coronavirus pandemic rendering many schools incapable of holding normal classes, many schools are transitioning to a distance learning model with classes held over video conferencing platform zoom. Unfortunately, many students who attend Title One schools find themselves unable to purchase the necessary technology to thrive in these classes. For this reason, we have partnered with many Title One schools in the San Francisco Bay Area to raise money to purchase refurbished laptops for these students to use during these uncertain times. It is essential that we make sure no student is left behind soley because of their financial situation.
